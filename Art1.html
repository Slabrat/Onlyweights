A Moment in History

Any Strongman afficionado will tell you that Eddie Hall was an absolute monster. In both 2016 and 2017 Eddie had the world of strength training by the throat.

Many recall the controversy ignited by one Hafthor Bjornsson in 2017’s World Strongest man after he voiced that he had been cheated for reps on the Viking Press, and that Eddie Halls win of 2017 World’s Strongest Man was a fraud. I remember watching, and I know that Eddie Hall won the entire championship by a wide margin, his own performance on the Viking Press opposite to Bjornsson was stellar, in fact he didn’t even appear winded when he merely completed a single additional repetition of the event to beat Bjornsson’s score and take the title of the Strongest Man in the World.

But as phenomenal as this performance was it was far from Eddie’s greatest feat. Many will tell you about his simultaneous capture of the Logpress and Overhead Press world record in that timeframe. But they all paled in comparison to Eddie’s Greatest Achievement.

Deemed impossible during that time, a Deadlift of 1100 pounds was unthinkable. Eddie, in 2015 not only insisted that he could do it, but that he could accomplish this feat in only a year. The year long timeframe may not sound overly impressive, but weight is exponentially harder to lift, the heavier it gets. Any strength enthusiast can tell you that improving from a bench press of 315 lbs to 405 lbs is far more difficult and time-consuming that the initial trip of 225 pound to 315 pounds.

Yet Eddie managed, through several dramatic means, to lift 500kg or 1102 pounds. A monumental feat. He would later claim that he had been able to haul this great weight by imagining the lives of his children in danger before him. The video on the right shows Eddie pulling this weight. It is a video I am fond of, for it was what inspired me to start my own strength training journey. I hope you all enjoy it as I did, it is the sole reason of why I write this article, to capture the immense wonder of a grown man witnessing the nigh-impossible. A heroic feat. We get so few of those these days.
