A Stranglehold on Modern Strongman

Tom Stoltman, nicknamed “The Albatross,” has been a force to be reckoned with in the World’s Strongest Man Competition. While relatively new on the Strongman scene -to me, of course - this madman has demolished stiff competition. He has had daring head to head matchups with the likes of Aleksii Novikov, 2020’s World’s Strongest man, or Brian Shaw himself, a living legend and four-time World’s Strongest man.

We cannot forget Tom’s brother Luke one his four brothers, the two-time Europe’s Strongest Man, who has similarly made himself known on the world stage, amusingly he runs a YouTube channel for the pair. But what folks won’t tell you is that Tom, the three-time World’s Strongest Man, was diagnosed with autism at the age of 5.

His life was always dominated by a passion for physical sport. Football was Tom’s gateway into the sport of Strongman, and he played until he found his fondness for weightlifting at the age of 16. By 18 he won his first Strongest Man competition.

Today he is the Strongest Man on the planet, with an equipped deadlift of 948 pounds and an overall powerlifting total of 1995 pounds it is no wonder how he has given so many electric performances in competition recently. There is some worry, as recent injuries have called Tom away from the world stage toward other ventures like the Arnold Classic.
