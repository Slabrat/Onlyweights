Being Great

When I think of a “complete” strength athlete, there’s only one name that comes to mind. “Gigantor” or his real name, Brian Shaw. A four-time champion of the World’s Strongest Man competition and many others, Brian Shaw has been THE mainstay in this sport.

While he has pretty much retired from the World’s competition, he has branded a competition of his own under his brand Shawstrength. Now I could GUSH about this man’s lifts, successes and heartbreaks all day, but there is one aspect of this man that I absolutely MUST discuss. Brian Shaw is without a doubt one of the most wholesome individuals you will ever meet. I could not think of a better example without resorting to religious text.

Now many of you may wish to discuss the insane elephant bar deadlift, or that time he proved himself utterly impervious and unbothered by a two-hundred pound steel keg smashing into him after a twenty-foot drop, but they wouldn’t do the impact he has had on the sport justice. This man’s modest kindness has spoken far louder than any brash blow-hard in the sport. Even his callouts and confrontations are gentle, like the time he too-cordially challenged the “Vegan Strongman” to any event he wished in his home gym. Only crickets were heard.

I lied, there is one of his lifts I must gush about. Brian Shaw’s number two lament (his first was being unable to secure a fifth WSM title) was that he never was allowed to break the world Atlas Stone record in competition. Not because he had found his limit, but because they couldn’t make an Atlas Stone big enough. You heard that right, WSM was unable to craft a heavy enough stone for this man to lift on the world stage.

A seven hundred and thirty pound Atlas stone lift is an unreal feat. And this is coming from someone who struggles with two-hundred pound stones. Brian has reportedly lifted equivalents in his home gym. Being a six foot none behemoth certainly makes him far more able to handle the atypical handles of traditional strongman lifts. And not just the atlas stones. Brian also has a world record in lifting the Thomas Inch Dumbbell, a single-handed weight far too thick for mortal hands to hold. That is Brian Shaw, a “complete” Strongman, a “complete” example.
